# Understanding Skin Lesions: Recognizing Red Flags in Dermatology

Skin lesions are a common presentation in both primary and secondary care. These lesions often demonstrate characteristic features which can help distinguish between different pathologies. A comprehensive understanding of their typical presentations is essential for healthcare professionals to avoid missing critical conditions, such as skin cancer.

## Common Skin Lesion Characteristics

### Primary Features

- **Color**: Variations including red, brown, black, or pale.
- **Shape**: Round, oval, irregular, or asymmetrical.
- **Size**: Measurements often categorized as:
  - Small (`<6mm`)
  - Medium (`6mm - 10mm`)
  - Large (`>10mm`)

### **Types of Skin Lesions**

1. **Benign Lesions**
   - **Moles (Nevi)**: Typically brown and symmetrical. 
   - **Seborrheic Keratosis**: Waxy, raised, and often appears in older adults.

2. **Malignant Lesions**
   - **Melanoma**: Asymmetrical with irregular borders; colors may vary.
   - **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)**: Often appears as a red, scaly patch.

## Red Flag Features for Skin Cancer

Certain lesions exhibit **red flag** features that necessitate further evaluation:

- Size: Any lesion greater than `6mm`.
- Changes: Any lesion that changes in color or size.
- Shape: **Irregular borders** or asymmetry.
- Symptoms: Pruritus (itching), bleeding, or ulceration.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Skin cancer must not be missed. Be vigilant for any lesion demonstrating red flag features.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>Common Signs and Symptoms</summary>

- **Melanoma**
  - Asymmetrical shape
  - Border irregularity
  - Color variation (e.g., shades of brown, black, tan)
  
- **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)**
  - Red, scaly patch that may bleed or crust over
  
- **Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)**
  - Pearly or waxy bump
  - May have visible blood vessels

</details>

## Differential Diagnosis of Skin Lesions

| Lesion Type                  | Characteristics                               | Common Locations           |
|------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Melanocytic Nevi**         | Uniformly pigmented, may be raised            | Anywhere on the body        |
| **Basal Cell Carcinoma**     | Pearly papules, often ulcerated               | Head, neck, and arms        |
| **Squamous Cell Carcinoma**  | Red, crusted patches, may ulcerate            | Sun-exposed areas           |

## Diagnosis and Management

1. **Clinical Evaluation**
   - Full skin examination.
   - Patient history – duration, changes in appearance.

2. **Biopsy**
   - Excisional or incisional biopsy to confirm diagnosis.

3. **Management**
   - Surgical excision for malignant lesions.
   - Monitoring and potential removal for benign lesions.

<Callout emoji="💡">
**Note:** Utilize the ABCDE criteria (Asymmetry, Border, Color, Diameter, Evolving) for assessing moles.
</Callout>
