# Athlete's Foot (Tinea Pedis)

Athlete's foot, or **tinea pedis**, is a common fungal infection that affects the feet and toes, primarily caused by dermatophytes from the **_Trichophyton_** genus. It can present in several different subtypes, each with specific clinical features.

## Types of Athlete's Foot

### **Interdigital Type**
- Characterized by:
  - White or red, itchy, scaly, fissured skin between the toes.
  
### **Moccasin Type**
- Presents with:
  - Scaling and erythema on the plantar and lateral surfaces of the foot.

### **Vesicobullous Type**
- Features include:
  - Blisters on the arches of the foot.

<details>
<summary>Common Signs and Symptoms</summary>

- **Itching**: Often intense.
- **Pain**: Discomfort in affected areas.
- **Scaling**: Dry, flaky skin.
- **Fissuring**: Cracking of the skin, particularly between the toes.

</details>

## Diagnosis

Diagnosis of athleteâ€™s foot is primarily based on clinical signs and symptoms, including:

- **Symptoms**:
  - Itchy, painful feet.
  
- **Signs on examination**:
  - **Interdigital type**: Scaling, maceration, fissuring of toe web spaces.
  - **Moccasin type**: Scaling and erythema on plantar and lateral surfaces.
  - **Vesicobullous type**: Blisters on the arches.

_Correct identification of the subtype is crucial as it helps differentiate from other conditions, such as contact dermatitis, which may lead to _tinea incognito_ if mismanaged._

## Investigations

Skin sampling for fungal microscopy and/or culture should be considered in the following situations:

1. Severe disease.
2. Uncertainty in diagnosis.
3. Persistent infection despite appropriate antifungal treatment.

Skin swabs may be indicated if a bacterial infection is suspected, particularly if it arises as a secondary condition to athleteâ€™s foot.

## Management (Prescribing)

### **Topical Treatment**
- A topical antifungal cream is recommended for all patients with athleteâ€™s foot. First-line treatments include:
  - **Imidazoles** (e.g., clotrimazole, miconazole).
  - **Terbinafine**.

### **Co-Prescribing**
- Mild potency topical steroids may be considered if there are prominent inflammatory changes present.

### **Oral Antifungal Therapy**
- Reserved for severe cases of tinea pedis or when there is concurrent infection of other body parts.

## Management (Non-Prescribing)

### **Self-Care Advice**
Patients should be provided both verbal and written instructions regarding self-care strategies:

- Change socks frequently.
- Meticulously dry feet and toes, especially between the toes.
- Avoid occlusive footwear.
- Wear protective footwear in communal facilities.
