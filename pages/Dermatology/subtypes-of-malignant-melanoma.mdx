# Malignant Melanoma

## Overview

Malignant melanoma is an **uncontrolled proliferation of melanocytes**, primarily driven by **excessive UVB light exposure**. Understanding the risk factors, subtypes, and diagnostic criteria is crucial for effective management and treatment.

## Risk Factors

Key risk factors for malignant melanoma include:

- **DNA repair defects**, e.g., xeroderma pigmentosum
- **Sun exposure**, especially in fair-skinned individuals
- **Use of tanning beds**
- **Immune suppression** 

## Subtypes

The main subtypes of malignant melanoma include:

1. **Superficial Spreading Melanoma**
2. **Nodular Melanoma**
3. **Lentigo Maligna Melanoma**
4. **Acral Lentiginous Melanoma**

Other rare forms include:

- **Desmoplastic Melanoma**
- **Amelanotic Melanoma**
- **Ocular Melanoma**

## Diagnosis

Diagnosis of malignant melanoma is typically made **histologically** following an **excision biopsy**. 

### ABCDE Rule

Features of melanoma are often described using the **ABCDE rule**:

| Feature               | Description                                    |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Asymmetry**        | Uneven shape of the mole                      |
| **Border Irregularity** | Edges that are notched, uneven, or blurred   |
| **Colour Variation**  | Multiple colors or shades within the lesion   |
| **Large Diameter**    | Greater than `6mm`                            |
| **Evolution**         | Changes in size, shape, color, or new lesions |

## Treatment

Treatment for malignant melanoma typically involves:

- **Wide Local Excision**: Removal of the melanoma with some surrounding tissue.
- **Sentinel Node Biopsy**: Assessment of lymph nodes for the spread of melanoma.
- **Adjuvant Therapy**: Additional treatment options as recommended, based on specific cases (See NICE guidance on melanoma treatment for more information). 

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Early detection and treatment of malignant melanoma are critical to improving prognosis and survival rates.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>Common Signs and Symptoms</summary>

- **Chest Pain**
  - Crushing or pressure-like
  - May radiate to left arm, jaw, or back
  - Often accompanied by sweating

- **Respiratory**
  - Shortness of breath
  - Rapid breathing
  - Orthopnea

- **Other Symptoms**
  - Nausea and vomiting
  - Fatigue
  - Light-headedness
  - Anxiety

</details>
