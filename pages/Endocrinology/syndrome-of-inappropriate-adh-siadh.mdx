# Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone Secretion (SIADH)

Syndrome of inappropriate ADH (SIADH) is a clinical syndrome characterized by **hypotonic hyponatraemia**, **high urine osmolality**, and **euvolaemia**. 

The condition arises from either increased secretion or responsiveness to the hormone **ADH** (antidiuretic hormone), which is secreted by the posterior pituitary. A diagnosis of SIADH is made only after excluding other potential causes of hyponatraemia.

## Etiology

The possible **aetiologies** of SIADH include:

- **Medications** 
  - SSRIs
  - Amitriptyline
  - Amiodarone
  - Chemotherapeutic agents
  - NSAIDs
  - Carbamazepine
  - Certain anaesthetic agents

- **Pulmonary Disease**
  - Infections
  - Cancers (small cell lung cancer)

- **Other Malignancies**
  - Lymphomas
  - Sarcomas
  - Gastrointestinal malignancies

- **Neurological Disease**
  - Trauma 

## Diagnosis

SIADH is primarily a clinical diagnosis achieved through the exclusion of other causes and the demonstration of the following key features:

1. **Hypotonic Hyponatraemia**
2. **High Urine Osmolality**
3. **Euvolaemia**

### Supporting Lab Results

Other symptoms may include:

- **Neurological**
  - Altered mental status
  - Headaches
  - Seizures
  - Comas

- **Gastrointestinal**
  - Nausea
  - Vomiting

## Investigations

The most appropriate **first-line investigations** include:

- Serum Osmolality
- Urine Osmolality 
- Serum Urea
- Serum Sodium

Further investigations may include:

- Serum Cortisol (to rule out adrenal insufficiency)
- Thyroid Function Tests (for hypothyroidism)

## Management (Prescribing)

When managing a patient with SIADH, focus on the following three main goals:

1. **Management of Sodium**
2. **Treatment of Underlying Cause**
3. **Ongoing Therapy** (in cases of chronic SIADH)

### Management of Sodium:

- Hypertonic saline and fluid restriction
- Furosemide if at risk of volume overload
- In some cases, **ADH receptor antagonists** (e.g., tolvaptan) may be used

### Treatment of Underlying Condition:

- Discontinue any medications causing SIADH
- Treat infections with antibiotics if indicated

### Ongoing Treatment:

- Continued fluid restriction
- Occasionally, ADH receptor agonists may be indicated (e.g., tolvaptan)

## Management (Non-Prescribing)

Fluid restriction and patient education are crucial for managing long-term and persistent SIADH. Patient compliance should be regularly reinforced.
