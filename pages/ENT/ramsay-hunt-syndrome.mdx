# Ramsay-Hunt Syndrome: Overview and Management

Ramsay-Hunt syndrome, also known as **herpes zoster oticus**, is due to the reactivation of the **varicella-zoster virus** in the 7th cranial nerve ganglion. The virus remains dormant; however, it can reactivate and spread to the facial nerve. This condition commonly occurs in **elderly patients**.

## Differential Diagnosis

While **Bellâ€™s palsy** can be a common differential diagnosis (noted for sudden onset of facial paralysis), **acoustic neuroma** can present similarly, featuring:

- Tinnitus
- Hearing loss
- Facial nerve weakness

## Triggers for Reactivation

Reactivation of Ramsay-Hunt syndrome can be triggered by:

- **Stress**
- **Immunosuppression** (e.g., AIDS)
- **Malnutrition**
- **Elderly age**

## Diagnosis

Patients typically present with the following complaints:

- **Auricular pain**
- **Unilateral cranial nerve VII palsy**
- **Vertigo**
- **Tinnitus**
- **Vesicular rash** around the ear and mouth
- **Sensorineural deafness** (transient, but can be permanent)

The classical facial rash may be very painful, erythematous, and vesicular. In some instances, the rash can spread to the mouth, soft palate, or external ear canal. Diagnosis can be challenging as symptoms may not develop concurrently.

  - Facial weakness on one side
  - Vertigo

## Investigations

In Ramsay-Hunt syndrome, **PCR testing** can reveal varicella-zoster virus infection in vesicular fluid. Furthermore, patients with recurrent herpes zoster infections should be investigated for other potential triggers, including those mentioned above.

## Management (Non-Prescribing)

Management includes:

1. **Antiviral Therapy**
   - Oral antivirals such as **acyclovir** or **famciclovir**.
   - Initiation of therapy within `3 days` of onset often leads to better outcomes.
   - *Note: Some hearing loss or paralysis may be permanent*.

2. **Corticosteroids**
   - Used to reduce inflammation.

3. **Other Medications**
   - **Carbamazepine** for facial neuralgic pain.
   - **Antihistamines** or **anticholinergics** for vertigo suppression.
