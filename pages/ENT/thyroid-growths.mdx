# Thyroid Cancers and Thyroglossal Cysts

## Thyroid Cancers

There are several types of **thyroid cancers**, which are differentiated based on clinical characteristics and biopsy findings.

The **majority (90%)** of thyroid cancers are differentiated, meaning that the cells maintain features similar to normal thyroid gland cells. The two primary types of differentiated thyroid cancers are:

- **Papillary Thyroid Cancer** (80-85%)
  - More common in **women** and **younger people**
  - Usually **slow-growing**, but can spread to lymph nodes in the neck
  - Good overall **cure rate**
  
- **Follicular Thyroid Cancer** (5-10%)
  - More common in **middle-aged** individuals
  - More likely to spread to **distant sites**, but still has a good overall **cure rate**

The following are **rarer types** of thyroid cancers that are considered undifferentiated:

- **Medullary Thyroid Cancer** (3-10%)
  - May be *sporadic* or related to an inherited endocrine syndrome
  - Arises from parafollicular cells and produces **calcitonin**, often causing *diarrhea*
  - Can spread and has a poorer outlook than the differentiated cancers

- **Anaplastic Thyroid Cancer** (1-3%)
  - Grows very **quickly** and causes symptoms of local invasion
  - Has a **poor outlook**

- **Hürthle Cell Thyroid Cancer** (4%)
  - Hürthle cells are abnormal cells that develop in the thyroid gland and are sometimes grouped with **follicular cancers** due to their similarities 

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Early detection in thyroid cancers, especially differentiated types, significantly improves management and prognosis.
</Callout>

## Thyroglossal Cysts

**Thyroglossal cysts** are an embryological remnant of the thyroid gland due to incomplete closure of the thyroglossal duct. These cysts typically present in **children** or **young adults**. 

Key characteristics:
- Majority are situated in the **midline of the neck**
- Characterized by elevation on **swallowing** or protrusion of the **tongue**

### Complications:
- **Infection**: Can be treated with *antibiotics*
- **Cutaneous Fistulas**

### Management:
- Surgical excision is recommended for most cysts.

<details>
<summary>Common Symptoms of Thyroglossal Cysts</summary>

- Swelling in the midline of the neck
- Pain or discomfort during swallowing
- Possible discharge if infected
- Mobility changes during tongue movement

</details>
