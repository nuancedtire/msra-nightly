# Acute Sore Throat Management

Acute sore throats are a **common presentation** in primary care, with various underlying causes such as:

- **Pharyngitis**
- **Tonsillitis**
- **Laryngitis**
- **Epiglottitis**
- **Glandular fever**

## Overview of Causes

Most sore throat infections are **viral in origin** and typically self-limiting. Management is primarily supportive, often guided by **pharmacist advice**.

### Antibiotic Considerations

Antibiotics are **not indicated** for the majority of sore throat cases because:

- There is **no evidence** that they improve symptoms.
- They do not hasten recovery.

Patients frequently request antibiotic prescriptions. It is crucial to explain why they may not be necessary, focusing on the viral nature of most sore throats.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Delayed and immediate antibiotic prescriptions may be necessary if a bacterial cause is suspected — indicated by symptoms not resolving within the natural history of a viral illness or presence of significant systemic upset.
</Callout>

### Risk Assessment Tools

NICE recommends utilizing scoring tools to identify patients who may need antibiotics, including:

- **Centor Score**
- **FeverPAIN Score**

These tools aid in risk stratification for antibiotic necessity.

## Severe Throat Infections

Be aware of high-risk features associated with severe throat infections, particularly:

- **Epiglottitis**: This can cause upper airway obstruction, especially in children. Any suspected case requires **immediate hospitalization**.
  
### Referral Guidelines

Any patient presenting with:

- An acute sore throat
- Significant systemic upset

Should be referred to a hospital for thorough assessment, as this could signify a serious bacterial infection.

<details>
<summary>Common Signs and Symptoms</summary>

- **Chest Pain**
  - Crushing or pressure-like
  - May radiate to left arm, jaw, or back
  - Often accompanied by sweating
  
- **Respiratory**
  - Shortness of breath
  - Rapid breathing
  - Orthopnea

- **Other Symptoms**
  - Nausea and vomiting
  - Fatigue
  - Light-headedness
  - Anxiety

</details>
