# Pneumonia Treatment Decision-Making Based on CURB-65

## Introduction to CURB-65 Score

The CURB-65 score is a clinical tool used to guide treatment decisions in patients diagnosed with pneumonia. It assesses the severity of the condition, with one point assigned for each of the following criteria:

- **Confusion:** Confusion of new onset (Abbreviated Mental Test Score ≤ 8).
- **Urea:** Blood urea nitrogen > 7 mmol/L.
- **Respiratory Rate:** > 30 breaths per minute.
- **Blood pressure:** Systolic < 90 mmHg, and/or diastolic ≤ 60 mmHg.
- **Age:** ≥ 65 years.

## Empirical Antibiotic Therapy

Due to the frequent absence of microbiological diagnostic testing to pinpoint causative organisms in pneumonia, empirical antibiotic therapy is often employed. This approach covers both typical and atypical organisms and is based on the CURB-65 score:

- **Scores 0-2:** Treatment may include amoxicillin, possibly combined with a macrolide, a tetracycline, or a quinolone.
- **Scores 3-5:** More severe cases require an intravenous beta-lactam plus either a macrolide or a quinolone.

<Callout emoji="ℹ️">
**Note:** It is essential to consult hospital trust guidelines, as local resistance patterns can influence the selection of antibiotics.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>Detailed Pathophysiology of Pneumonia</summary>

Pneumonia is an inflammatory condition of the lung primarily affecting the alveoli. It is usually caused by infection with viruses, bacteria, or other microorganisms, and less commonly by other causes such as inhaled irritants. The severity of pneumonia can vary from mild to life-threatening.

</details>
