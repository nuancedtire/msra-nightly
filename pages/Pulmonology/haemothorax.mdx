# Hemothorax: Diagnosis and Management

A hemothorax, the accumulation of blood in the pleural space, mainly results from trauma or iatrogenic causes. Rarely, it may occur spontaneously. It's critical to assess for a potentially life-threatening massive hemothorax during the primary survey in chest trauma scenarios. While surgical intervention is required for severe cases, thoracostomy and chest drainage are standard treatments for most hemothoraces.

## Diagnosis and Investigations

Suspect a hemothorax in patients presenting with a history of chest trauma, accompanied by symptoms such as chest pain and dyspnea. Clinical examination may reveal:

- Diminished breath sounds
- Dullness upon percussion over the affected side
- Signs of hemodynamic instability

**Imaging Techniques:**

- **Chest X-ray:** May miss smaller effusions.
- **Ultrasound:** Preferred in emergencies for its superior sensitivity.

**Laboratory Confirmation:**

A hemothorax is confirmed when pleural fluid analysis shows a hematocrit level at least half that of peripheral blood, obtained through thoracentesis or chest drainage.

## Emergency Diagnosis and Management

### Massive Hemothorax

A massive hemothorax is a dire emergency, characterized by:

- Initial chest drain output of `>1500 mL` of blood
- Sustained blood loss of `>200 mL/hr` over `2-4 hours`

**Initial Steps:**

- Administer oxygen immediately
- Approach fluid resuscitation with caution
- Consider blood transfusion in a `1:1` ratio of red cells to plasma
- Administer tranexamic acid promptly

**Definitive Surgical Management:**

Promptly consult with a cardiothoracic team. Video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS) is preferred over traditional thoracotomy.

## Non-Prescribing Management

Most cases are managed by draining the blood via a chest drain, typically placed in the triangle of safety, to avert complications such as:

- Intrathoracic blood clots
- Empyema
- Pleural effusion
- Fibrothorax

## Prescribing Management

- **Antibiotics:** For prophylaxis or treatment of empyema.
- **Fibrinolytic Therapy:** To address clots in the pleural space, although their use must be balanced against the risk of inducing a hemothorax.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Cautious use of fibrinolytics is advised due to the potential risk of causing or exacerbating a hemothorax.
</Callout>
