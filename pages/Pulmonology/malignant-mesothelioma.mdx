# Malignant Mesothelioma: A Comprehensive Overview

## Introduction

Malignant mesothelioma is an aggressive cancer originating from the mesothelial cells lining the lung, abdomen, pericardium, or tunica vaginalis. Predominantly, it manifests as malignant pleural mesothelioma. Notably, it's closely linked to asbestos exposure, with a latency period ranging from 20 to 40 years.

## Diagnosis of Malignant Mesothelioma

### History and Risk Factors

- **Age of Onset:** Symptoms typically emerge between the ages of 60-90, often indicating advanced disease.
- **Environmental Exposure:** A detailed history including potential asbestos exposure is vital for diagnosis.
- **Other Risk Factors:**
  - Family history, specifically BAP-1 gene mutations.
  - History of radiation exposure.

### Occupational Assessment

- Investigate occupational history for high-risk jobs, such as:
  - Shipyard work
  - Construction
  - Maintenance
  - Vehicle brake mechanics
  - Insulation manufacturing
  - Tile, shingle, gasket, or textile production

### Symptomatology

- **Intrathoracic Symptoms:**
  - Breathlessness
  - Persistent dry cough
  - Chest discomfort
  
- **General Symptoms:**
  - Fatigue
  - Lack of appetite
  - Weight reduction

### Physical Examination

- Signs associated with pleural effusions:
  - Muted breath sounds
  - Limited chest expansion
  - Percussive dullness on the affected side
- Abdominal swelling may suggest ascites from peritoneal involvement or pleural disease extension.

## Investigations

### Initial Imaging

- A chest X-ray may reveal:
  - Unilateral pleural effusion
  - Pleural thickening
  - Reduced lung volumes
  - Lower zone interstitial fibrosis or pleural calcification
  
<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Note:** Despite its utility, a chest X-ray may not detect finer details, making further investigation essential even if initial findings are inconclusive.
</Callout>

### Advanced Imaging and Procedures

- A contrast-enhanced CT scan of the chest and upper abdomen is recommended for those with a high clinical suspicion of mesothelioma.
- Thoracentesis to classify the effusion and analyze pleural fluid cytology.
- Video-assisted thorascopic surgery (VATS) to obtain biopsies for definitive diagnosis.

### Additional Diagnostic Tools

- **PET Scanning:**
  - Conducted post-diagnosis to evaluate the extent of disease spread.
- **Pulmonary Function Tests:**
  - Measurement of FEV1 and DLCO is essential for surgical candidates.

## Management

### Chemotherapy

- **Neoadjuvant Therapy:** Administered preoperatively for resectable mesothelioma.
- **Palliative Care:** Offered to patients with inoperable or recurrent mesothelioma.
- **Effective Agents:**
  - Cisplatin (with possible side effects like nephrotoxicity and nausea)
  - Carboplatin (preferred for safety and administration ease)
  - Pemetrexed

### Surgical Intervention

#### Definitive Surgical Methods

1. Extra-pleural pneumonectomy involves removing the pleura, affected lung, pericardium, and hemidiaphragm.
2. Pleurectomy with decortication entails removing both the parietal and visceral pleura.

### Radiotherapy

- Adjuvant radiotherapy post-surgery or to alleviate symptoms from tumor growth.

### Palliative Procedures

- Thoracocentesis and pleurodesis (using talc) can help alleviate symptoms by preventing fluid re-accumulation.
