# Understanding and Managing Neonatal Jaundice

Neonatal jaundice is a common medical condition affecting over 50% of newborn infants. Classification of this condition can be based on several parameters, one of which is the onset time. The key distinction lies between physiological jaundice and pathological jaundice.

## Classification of Neonatal Jaundice

### Physiological Jaundice

Physiological jaundice is part of the normal adaptation process from fetal to infant life.

### Pathological Jaundice

Pathological jaundice arises due to an underlying disease process. It is important to note that jaundice appearing within the first 24 hours of life is always pathological.

#### Causes of Pathological Jaundice

- **Hemolysis**: This is a common cause of jaundice within the first 24 hours. Conditions such as Rhesus hemolytic disease, ABO incompatibility, and G6PD deficiency can lead to hemolytic anemia.
- **Breastfeeding and Dehydration**: These factors can cause jaundice between 24 hours and two weeks of age.
- **Infection and Hemolytic Disease**: These are potential causes for jaundice occurring between 24 hours and two weeks of age.

Jaundice that persists beyond the first two weeks of life is known as **prolonged jaundice**. This could be due to physiological causes, infection, or hemolysis. However, it may also be caused by obstructive gastrointestinal conditions such as pyloric stenosis, biliary atresia, or neonatal hepatitis.

## Management of Neonatal Jaundice

The management strategy for jaundice includes the following steps:

1. **Clinical Assessment**: Evaluate the severity of the condition through a clinical examination.
2. **Bilirubin Measurement**: Measure the serum bilirubin levels. Differentiating between conjugated and unconjugated bilirubin can help ascertain the underlying cause.
3. **Treatment**: This involves addressing the root cause and managing the jaundice symptoms. The treatment plan can range from ensuring adequate hydration to phototherapy, and in severe cases, exchange transfusion may be necessary.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Understanding the causes of neonatal jaundice is critical to identify serious cases and manage them effectively. If left untreated, severe cases caused by hemolysis and infection can lead to disability and even death.
</Callout>
