# Familial Hypercholesterolaemia: Understanding the Genetic Lipid Disorder

Familial hypercholesterolaemia is a **genetic condition** marked by excessively high cholesterol levels. According to NICE guidelines, a **total cholesterol level** of more than `7.5 mmol/L` is a significant indicator of this disease, as such levels are uncommon due to lifestyle factors alone.

## Genetic Characteristics

- **Heterogeneity**: The condition involves numerous causative mutations.
- **Inheritance Pattern**: Most commonly inherited in an **autosomal dominant** manner.
- **Family History**: Usually associated with a strong family history of coronary heart disease due to **atherosclerosis pathogenesis**.

## Diagnostic Criteria

### High Cholesterol Levels

- **Total Cholesterol**: Levels exceeding `7.5 mmol/L` are suspicious for familial hypercholesterolaemia.

### Family History

- **Early Coronary Heart Disease**: A family history of early onset cardiac events (before 60 years in a first-degree relative) suggests familial hypercholesterolaemia.

<summary>Additional Information: Referral and Testing</summary>

- **Genetic Testing**: Referral for genetic testing is recommended for individuals meeting the above criteria.
- **NICE Guidelines**: Follow recommendations outlined by NICE for proper identification and management.
