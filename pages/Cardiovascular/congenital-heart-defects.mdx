# Overview of Fallot's Tetralogy

Fallot's Tetralogy is a congenital heart defect characterized by four distinct anatomical features. It is the most prevalent cause of cyanotic congenital heart disease in infants.

## Key Features

1. **Pulmonary Stenosis**: A narrowing of the right ventricular outflow tract, leading to reduced blood flow to the lungs.
2. **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy**: Thickening of the muscular walls of the right ventricle as a result of increased workload.
3. **Overriding Aorta**: The aorta is positioned directly over a ventricular septal defect, receiving blood from both the right and left ventricles.
4. **Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)**: A hole in the septum between the heart's two lower chambers, allowing oxygen-poor and oxygen-rich blood to mix.

## Clinical Presentation

- **Age of Onset**: Typically presents between `1-2 months` of age.
- **Symptoms**: Episodes of cyanosis (bluish discoloration of the skin due to lack of oxygen) can occur, which may lead to syncope (fainting) and seizures if severe.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Severe cyanotic episodes require immediate medical attention to prevent potential complications such as seizures.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>Detailed Pathophysiology</summary>

Fallot's Tetralogy results from a combination of anatomical defects that affect the flow of blood through the heart. The restricted pulmonary outflow due to pulmonary stenosis leads to right ventricular hypertrophy, as the heart works harder to pump blood. The overriding aorta receives blood from both ventricles due to the ventricular septal defect, which contributes to the mixing of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood, resulting in systemic cyanosis.

</details>

## Management

- **Surgical Repair**: The definitive treatment involves surgical correction, typically within the first year of life, to repair the defects and improve blood flow.
- **Monitoring and Support**: Continuous monitoring of vital signs and supportive care is essential to manage symptoms until surgery can be performed.
