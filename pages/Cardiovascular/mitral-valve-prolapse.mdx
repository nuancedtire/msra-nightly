# Understanding Mitral Valve Prolapse (MVP)

Mitral Valve Prolapse (MVP) is a prevalent heart valve disorder, affecting approximately `2.4%` of the US population. It occurs when the mitral valve leaflet(s) bulge into the left atrium during systole. Although many patients remain asymptomatic, MVP can lead to complications such as mitral regurgitation, infective endocarditis, and in rare cases, sudden cardiac death.

## Diagnosis

**Risk Factors:**

- Connective tissue disorders, including Marfan syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome, osteogenesis imperfecta, and pseudoxanthoma elasticum
- Family history of MVP
- Thin body build

**Symptoms:**

While many individuals with MVP do not experience symptoms, some may present with:

- Palpitations
- Syncope
- Atypical chest pain
- Anxiety

**Auscultation Findings:**

- **Mid-systolic click**
- **Late systolic murmur**: Medium to high pitched, varying in length, indicates mitral regurgitation
- Changes in preload and afterload can alter the timing of the click and murmur:
  - Decreased preload (e.g., standing, Valsalva maneuver) or increased afterload (e.g., amyl nitrate) causes the click and murmur to occur earlier in systole.
  - Increased preload (e.g., squatting) or decreased afterload (e.g., handgrip maneuvers) causes them to occur later in systole.

## Investigations

- **Echocardiogram**: The definitive diagnostic tool. It identifies leaflet prolapse of `>2mm` above the annulus level in systole, especially in the long-axis parasternal or apical view on 2D imaging.
- **Holter or ambulatory event monitoring**: Used to detect arrhythmias, especially if the patient presents with palpitations or syncope.

## Management (Prescribing)

Typically, asymptomatic patients do not require treatment. However, certain medications are prescribed based on specific conditions:

- **Aspirin (75-300mg orally once daily)**:
  - Indicated for patients with atrial fibrillation, ischemic stroke, or a history of transient ischemic attack
  - Postoperative use after mitral valve repair

- **Warfarin (2-10mg orally once daily, titrate dose to target INR of 2-3)**:
  - Prescribed for patients with previous systemic embolism or atrial fibrillation, including in the postoperative period

- **Beta-blockers**: May alleviate symptoms such as palpitations, anxiety, or atypical chest pain.

- **Heart Failure Management**: For severe mitral regurgitation where surgery is not an option, patients may benefit from medications like digoxin, vasodilators, angiotensin blockers, and diuretics.

## Management (Non-prescribing)

- **Lifestyle Modifications**: To alleviate palpitations, patients are advised to avoid stress, stimulants (e.g., caffeine, nicotine), and alcohol.

- **Surgical Intervention**: Mitral valve repair is recommended for MVP patients with severe mitral regurgitation who exhibit:
  - Left ventricular dysfunction
  - Pulmonary hypertension
  - Atrial fibrillation

  Percutaneous mitral valve repair is a less preferred alternative, reserved for patients unsuitable for surgery or those with severe mitral regurgitation and heart failure resistant to medical treatment.

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Continuous monitoring of vital signs is crucial during treatment and management of MVP.
</Callout>
