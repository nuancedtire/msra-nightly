# Tension Headaches: Diagnosis and Management

Tension headaches are the most prevalent type of headache among patients. However, the exact mechanisms causing tension headaches are not fully understood.

## Pathophysiology

- The underlying pathophysiology of tension headaches is not well-defined.

<details>
<summary>Detailed Pathophysiology</summary>

The specific cause of tension headaches remains elusive to researchers, indicating the need for further scientific study to understand the mechanisms involved.

</details>

## Diagnosis of Tension Headaches

### Clinical Diagnosis

- Diagnosing tension headaches relies on clinical assessment.
- Additional tests are generally unnecessary unless other conditions must be ruled out.

### Patient Presentation

- Symptoms include a tight, band-like headache that is diffuse and of mild to moderate intensity.
- Duration ranges from a few hours to several days.
- Daily activities are typically unaffected.
- Most commonly occurs in adolescents and adults.

### Classification

- **Episodic Tension Headaches**: Occur `<15 days` per month.
- **Chronic Tension Headaches**: Occur `>15 days` per month.

### Common Triggers

- Stress and anxiety
- Fatigue
- Dehydration
- Skipping meals
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Poor posture

## Investigations

- Routine tests are not required for confirming tension headaches.

**Note:** Red flags in symptoms may necessitate further investigations to exclude more serious conditions.

### Red Flag Symptoms

- Abrupt onset
- Exceptional severity
- Accompanied by confusion, neck stiffness, photophobia, fever, nausea, or vomiting
- Preceding significant head injury
- Associated with neurological deficits such as weakness, sensory changes, or speech difficulties

## Management of Tension Headaches

### Prescribing Treatment

- Simple analgesics are typically effective.
  
<Callout emoji="ðŸ’Š">
**First-Line Options:** The National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) advises using paracetamol or ibuprofen.
</Callout>

- Consider codeine for unrelieved pain by other analgesics, but be cautious of potential medication-overuse headaches.
  
<Callout emoji="âš ï¸">
**Warning:** Codeine is not generally recommended for routine use.
</Callout>

- For chronic tension headaches, low-dose amitriptyline may be used off-label as a preventative treatment.

### Non-Prescribing Treatment

- Encourage patients to maintain a headache diary tracking occurrence, frequency, severity, and triggers.

#### Lifestyle Modifications

- Engage in regular physical activity and relaxation techniques.
- Adjust diet to avoid known triggers.
- Practice good sleep hygiene.
- Focus on maintaining proper posture.
- Ensure adequate hydration.

<details>
<summary>Additional Non-Pharmacological Interventions</summary>

For persistent chronic tension headaches, NICE suggests considering up to 10 acupuncture sessions over 5-8 weeks.

</details>
