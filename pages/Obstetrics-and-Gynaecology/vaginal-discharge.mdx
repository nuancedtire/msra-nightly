# Understanding Recurrent Candidiasis and Genitourinary Disease

## Recurrent Candidiasis: A Comprehensive Overview

### Definition and Diagnosis

Recurrent candidiasis is characterized by:

- Having **four or more episodes** within a year.
- Episodes exhibit at least partial symptom resolution in between.

### Significance and Underlying Conditions

- Persistent or poorly responsive candidiasis may signal an **immunosuppressive illness**.
- It's crucial to investigate for conditions that compromise immune function.

### Screening and Initial Assessment

Consider evaluating for:

- **Diabetes Mellitus**: Perform a blood glucose test as a cost-effective initial screen.
- **HIV**: Despite the absence of high-risk demographics, testing should not be ignored.

### Predisposing Factors

Screening should include factors such as:

- Antibiotic usage
- Risky sexual behaviors
- Exposure to oestrogen

<Callout emoji="⚠️">
**Warning:** Overlooking underlying diseases in recurrent candidiasis can lead to misdiagnosis and inadequate treatment.
</Callout>

## Genitourinary Disease: Recognition and Management

### Presentation and Symptoms

Genitourinary diseases can manifest as:

- Localized genital symptoms
- Urinary and abdominal complaints
- Systemic symptoms

Both men and women can experience:

- Skin changes
- Genital pain and swelling
- Dysuria
- Discharge

### Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

- STIs like gonorrhoea and chlamydia might be asymptomatic.
- Diagnosis often relies on screening and contact tracing.

### Importance of Awareness

- Common in sexually active individuals
- Present in various healthcare settings, including primary care and emergency departments.

### Impact on Quality of Life

Genitourinary conditions can lead to:

- Infertility
- Sexual dysfunction
- Social stigma

Appropriate identification and treatment are vital to prevent long-term complications.
